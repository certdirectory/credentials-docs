# Creating an Account

You don't strictly need an account to *receive* a credential — anyone with the verification link can already see it. But creating an account lets you:

- **Claim** credentials so they're permanently linked to you.
- Build a **public profile** at `/u/your-slug` showcasing all your credentials.
- **Import** Open Badges 3.0 credentials you've earned on other platforms.
- **Export** your wallet as a Verifiable Presentation (VP).

## Sign up

1. Go to [credentials.certdirectory.io/register](https://credentials.certdirectory.io/register).
2. Choose either:
    - **Email + password** — enter your first name, last name, email, and a password (must include upper & lower case, a number, and a symbol; minimum length enforced).
    - **Continue with Google** — uses your Google account; email is auto-verified.
3. If you signed up with email, you'll receive a **verification email**. Click the link to activate your account, then return and sign in.

!!! tip "If you don't see the verification email"
    Check spam, then use **Resend verification** on the login screen — you can do this 3 times every 15 minutes per email.

## Sign in

- Email + password, or **Continue with Google**.
- If you try to sign in before verifying your email, you'll get a clear `EMAIL_NOT_VERIFIED` message with a link to resend the verification email.

## After signing in

You're taken to your **Dashboard** at `/dashboard`, which shows:

- **Pending credentials** — credentials issued to your email that you haven't claimed yet.
- **Your credentials** — credentials you've claimed.
- **Organizations** — orgs you own or are a member of (if any).
- **Invitations** — pending invitations to join other organizations.

## Why claim credentials?

When a credential is *issued* to your email, it's already publicly verifiable. **Claiming** it (see [Claiming a Credential](claiming-a-credential.md)) does two things:

1. Permanently links it to your user account, so it appears in your dashboard and on your public profile.
2. Lets you decide whether to display it on your public profile.
